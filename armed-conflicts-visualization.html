Lives Lost in Armed Conflicts — 2024

An interactive data visualization examining the human cost of armed conflict through publicly available data.

Tableau Data Journalism Conflict Datasets Interactive Design
01

Context

This project explores lives lost in armed conflicts in 2024, using publicly available data to surface patterns, scale, and geographic distribution of fatalities. The objective was not to provide political analysis, but to present the data in a way that allows viewers to grasp the magnitude of loss and compare conflicts without abstraction.

Tools & Role

Tools: Tableau, public conflict datasets

Role: Concept, analysis, design, and development

02

Design & System Thinking

Focus on Comparability, Not Commentary

The visualization was designed to allow users to compare conflicts across regions, relative scale of lives lost, and distribution patterns over time and geography.

  • No subjective framing - Data speaks for itself without narrative overlays
  • Regional comparisons - Side-by-side conflict analysis
  • Scale visualization - Magnitude representation without distortion
  • Temporal patterns - Distribution over time and geography

Visual Restraint by Design

Given the subject matter, visual decisions were intentionally conservative to ensure focus remained on the data rather than dramatic effects.

  • Limited color palette - Minimal, respectful color usage
  • Clean layouts - Uncluttered, readable design
  • Decorative minimalism - Avoidance of visual embellishments
  • Clarity over spectacle - Accuracy prioritized over dramatic effect

Interactive Exploration Over Static Storytelling

Rather than prescribing a single narrative, the visualization enables independent exploration and organic movement between overview and detail.

  • User-driven exploration - Independent conflict investigation
  • Flexible filtering - Focus on regions of interest
  • Organic navigation - Overview to detail transitions
  • Interpretive freedom - No forced conclusions or narratives

Accessibility and Clarity

The dashboard was structured to be readable without prior context, with clear labels, intuitive interactions, and minimal technical terminology.

04

Visuals

Key Takeaway

Data journalism is most effective when it reduces abstraction. By presenting conflict-related data in a clear, comparative, and interactive form, this project aims to help viewers better understand scale—without editorializing or sensationalizing the underlying data.
